The Portuguese Speaking Chaplaincy in the Melbourne Archdiocese is a fervent active Catholic community, migrant in a foreign land, tries to provide pastoral, religious and spiritual assistance to people, favoring the preservation of traditions and devotions popular through the integration of liturgical meetings, employers and social events celebrations that mark the history of each country. It is noteworthy that in the Melbourne Archdiocese this Chaplaincy is composed on the one hand, by all countries whose official language is Portuguese, such as Portugal, Brazil, East Timor, Mozambique, Angola, Cape Verde and Macao. While on the other hand, there are three ethnic groups constantly attending to the Chaplaincy: Portugal, East Timor and Brazil, which have their consistency in boiling tradition of the Portuguese, the Asian spirit of the East Timorese and the sporty charisma of Brazilians. All this without forgetting the new generations, those who were born in Australia and accompanying during important events or parties.
